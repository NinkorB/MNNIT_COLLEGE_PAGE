Vision

To produce globally competitive technical manpower with sound knowledge of theory and practice, with a commitment to serve the society and to foster cutting edge research in Electrical Engineering pertaining to the problems currently faced by the country and the world.

Mission

  • To develop state of the art lab facilities for research and consultancy.
  • To develop infrastructure and procurement of cutting edge tools/equipment.
  • To develop relevant content for quality teaching.

About the Department

The Electrical Engineering Department was established in 1961 with the goal of producing high-quality technical manpower and promoting research. It began with a Bachelor of Engineering (BE) program in 1961, followed by postgraduate programs in Electrical Machines/Power Systems/Control Systems in 1970-71. In 1976-77, the department introduced the first BE degree in Computer Science in the country, followed by the BE in Electronics in 1982-83. In 1986, the department also launched a Master's in Computer Applications (MCA) program. Today, the department offers B.Tech in Electrical Engineering and M.Tech and Ph.D. programs in Power Electronics & Drives, Control & Instrumentation, and Power Systems (available in Regular, Part-Time, and QIP categories). The department is equipped with six specialized labs and is involved in various research projects funded by MNRE, DST, and MHRD. It is also a recognized QIP center for Ph.D. programs for engineering and polytechnic faculty members.
