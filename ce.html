Vision

The Department of Civil Engineering is committed towards the excellence in education, research, and professional development of the students, faculty members, and staff so as to enable them to productively contribute towards the sustainable development and advancement of our country.

Mission

  • To educate students for excellence in Civil Engineering and national development.
  • To advance research in engineering and sustainable technologies.
  • To serve and enhance the Institute, profession, and society.

About the Department

The Department of Civil Engineering was established in 1961 with an initial intake of 30 students. The first Head of the Department was Prof. S.C. Jain. Initially, classes and labs were held at Allahabad Polytechnic before relocating to the main building in the third year. The department began with five laboratories and later expanded to include more. The intake increased to 70 students, and an M.Tech. program in Structural Engineering and Soil Mechanics was introduced in 1970. Over time, additional M.Tech. specializations were added, and new labs developed. Currently, the department offers four M.Tech. programs: Structural Engineering, Geotechnical Engineering, Environmental Engineering, and Transportation Engineering. It is also recognized as a QIP center for M.Tech. and Ph.D. programs. The department has 41 faculty members, with an intake of 121 B.Tech and 76 M.Tech students.
